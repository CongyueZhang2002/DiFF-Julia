# Strict Draft-vs-Code Audit for `DiFF_julia`

Overall result: the ratio observables `EE_EEC_AUT` and `SIDIS_EEC_AUT` are broadly consistent with the draft formulas after expected cancellation of common prefactors, but the public unpolarized observables and several notebook workflows do not match the draft strictly. The largest gaps are the unpolarized SIDIS path, the unpolarized `e^+e^-` normalization, and inconsistent notebook policies for replica sampling and lattice/non-lattice branches.

## Findings

1. `SIDIS_EEC` is not the draft's unpolarized SIDIS EEC. It uses the separate `D1_EEC_own` toy model, omits the draft prefactors, and depends on `p0`, `p1`, `p2` that are not defined in the inspected Julia source tree.
2. `EE_EEC` is not Eq. (13) from the draft. The implementation normalizes by the total charge sum and omits the `4 pi N_c alpha_em^2 / (3 Q^2)` prefactor, so it should be read as a fitted shape surrogate rather than the published observable.
3. The notebook policy for draft figures is inconsistent with the draft. The EIC and no-lattice scan notebooks do not share the same replica-count rule, and the EIC-named notebooks default to the no-lattice card only.
4. The asymmetry functions are the strongest match. `SIDIS_EEC_AUT` and `EE_EEC_AUT` do implement the expected charge-weighted ratios once common prefactors cancel.
5. The flavor completion step from JAM grids to full quark/antiquark vectors relies on implicit `\pi^+\pi^-` symmetry assumptions. That is reasonable for JAMDiFF, but it is not made explicit in the draft excerpt.

## Draft Ambiguities And Validation Notes

- The draft PDF pass already flagged two text ambiguities that should remain separate from implementation errors: the duplicated delta notation in Eq. (8), and the positivity paragraph's `alpha/beta` wording when the actual bounds are on `(a,b)`.
- `Main/a_b_uniform_area_100.xlsx` contains `100` usable `(a,b)` pairs. A local parse performed during this audit found `0` violations of the stated positivity constraints.
- `predict_EEC.ipynb` contains an explicit positivity-enforcement helper for the unpolarized fit, but the AUT scan notebooks rely on prevalidated scan inputs rather than rechecking the bounds programmatically.
